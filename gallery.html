With the exception of the very first, this selection of Fred's masterpieces comes from the 'experimental' period of her youth, a time during which she was heavily influenced by the great master George.

Although the Gallery will generally be reserved for the exhibition of Fred's works, on occasion certain particularly influential pieces by other artists will also be highlighted in the Gallery. The premier place of such exceptions is here granted to the great master. The artist formerly and currently known as George (although also known as Sam) presents herself, with a little help from Fred.

The photos that follow, also produced in the highly creative atmosphere of the experimental period, feature the (in)famous Natchitoches cemetery in which Sally Field (and many others) found their passion.

Over the years, Fred has frequented the sultry city of New Orleans to seek its inspiration and pleasures.

This exhibit is representative of the Baton Rouge period, which is marked by an increasing interest in dark imagery and a near obsession with contrast.
