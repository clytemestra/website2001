Fred's Life

I had the ill-fortune of being born in Morgan City, La. My parents are both from rural towns in the central part of south Louisiana where parents still teach their children French and coffee is a social requisite. I don't know why they didn't stay in this wonderfully culture-rich community. In any case, perhaps seeking a brighter future, they succumbed to the lure of the big money rumored to be had in the oil industry and moved to Morgan City in the early sixties.

The details of my life in Morgan City are already well-known to those who have any business knowing them.

In the spring of my sophomore year at Morgan City High School, my application to the Louisana School for Math, Science, and the Arts (LSMSA) was accepted. LSMSA is one of those strange hybrids of public and private schools--a publicly funded boarding school to which one must pay tution to attend. Thrilled at the prospect of escape, I pounced.

Encouraged to apply by my Latin teacher, I won a scholarship at the Catholic University of America. Not one to refuse money, I enrolled. And so I spent my first year of college in Washington, D.C., but after discovering that I wasn't rich, religious, or from New Jersey, I left to pursue an exciting career in the hazardous waste industry.

After about a year or so of getting to know 40 CFR and OSHA regulations and answering phones, I was ready to take on Latin and Greek again and enrolled part-time at LSU. Commuting 80 miles between work and school is something I would not recommend.

I moved to Baton Rouge in 1994, got married, and enrolled full-time. Between 1994 and 1998, I quit school entirely several times, got divorced, and read lots of good books. In August of 1998, I finally received my bachelor of arts in Latin from Louisiana State University, in Baton Rouge, La. (along with Jason).

After graduation and after living in Baton Rouge for four years in my case and eight in Jason's, we decided, rather precipitously, to move to Fayetteville, Ark., where his grandmother and aunt had lived for several years. After about two months of unemployment (during which I taught myself how to write poetry in meter, just for fun), I suddenly had two part-time jobs, one as an editorial assistant at a planetary science journal housed in the basement of the chemistry building at the University of Arkansas and one as a "bookseller" at the local Barnes and Noble.

I worked very hard and eventually got on full-time at guess which one.That's where I met Paul, who is Canadian by birth, whose last name is Benoit, and who knows what Cajuns are. This led to some furious family history research on both of our parts and the ambiguous discovery of a Marie Benoit in both. So, we're cousins, right? I don't know--dark hair, dark eyes, strange gothic hobbies. Seems obvious to me.
