Most of my life-long, and therefore true, friends live 1K+ miles away from me--no matter where I live.

George

A little over ten years ago, I arrived at the Louisiana School for Math, Science, and the Arts to find a tall (relative to me), skinny girl named Angela in my suite. With my usual cynicism, I immediately decided that she was entirely too gregarious for me. I knew not what the future held. George now lives in Boston in her very own house with her very own Jennifer. George and Jennifer have four cats who liked to be called Drat, Niblet, Raymond, and Choco. (And if she would send some pictures, like I told her to, you would be able to tell them hello.) George is also working towards a degree in Philosophy at UMass Boston and working on a book on Julia Tyler for a new First Ladies series being published by NOVA.

Joren

I also met a guy named Chris, who went to Berkeley and became a lawyer. Joren is happily newly married, and apparently enjoys helping people apply for U.S. citizenship. He's really bright guy with a lot of lofty ideals.

Cashman

Cashman finished MIT in 3 1/2 years and now has more money than God. He likes to travel, and is also happily newly married. Cashman has graduated from software engineer into management and is currently working to make sure that more spam can be sent to more poeple faster.

Paul

I met Paul while working at Meteoritics and Planetary Science in Fayetteville, Ark. Marie Benoit appears in both our family tree, so we must be related. Right? He likes to travel, too, and, like Cashman, has been to Antarctia on a scientific rock-gathering expedition. I find it quite amazing that I know two people who have been to the least-visited and, until recently, only uninhabited continent on the planet. But then again, my mind is easily boggled. By the way, he actually lives within the 1K mi. radius, but I guess it doesn't really matter how far away your friends live when you refuse to fly.
