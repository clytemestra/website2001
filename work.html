Labor me vocat. -- F. M. Wheelock

 

What do I do? I edit , I write, I rewrite, and I create web pages. (Although lately, I've been getting paid to hang out in the library and read books--not so bad, really.) I work for Bonnie Wheeler, editor of the journal Arthuriana and editor-in-chief of Scriptorium Press. So far, the pinnacle of my time with Bonnie has been the production of outlines and ancillary materials for a series of lectures on the world of King Arthur. You might think that a university professor would grok the importance of planning and managing projects, well, think again. I believe that one of the qualifications for any academic position is to have mastered the art of procrastination and the all-nighter.

Since March 2000, however, I've been moonlighting at McCaslin Associates, a local architectural firm, acting as web and marketing consultant. I designed and built their web site from scratch. I've also been busy writing copy for various other marketing projects.

Somehow, wherever I go, I end up working at places either directly or indirectly affiliated with a university. My office is in the basement of Dallas Hall on the SMU campus. This building isthe oldest on campus and originally housed all the classrooms and offices, the library, and the chapel.

What does the future hold for me at Scriptorium Press? Right now, I'm working on a second series of lectures and a book on T. H. White.
