NIH Workshop: Designing, Developing, and Preparing Research Diets

    Congratulations to Dr. Marlene Windhauser and the rest of the Metabolic Kitchen staff for their super-successful training workshop last month.
    "Designing, Preparing, and Delivering Research Diets," held once every two years, is sponsored by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute.  The workshop, which began this year on Sunday, June 8 and ended Wednesday, June 11,  focused on preparing for and maintaining metabolic diet studies.
    Through these workshops and successful studies like DASH, Penningtonâs Metabolic Kitchen is gaining recognition in the scientific community. "Other universities, when applying for grants, are saying that their staff was trained at the Pennington Center," said Dr. Windhauser.
    Forty-four researchers from California to Massachusetts came to the Pennington Center to learn how to conduct better nutrition studies and share research methods.
    "Pennington is well respected for the ability to offer a vast array of knowledge and for enhancing research in the area of nutrition. I feel Iâm learning from the best!"  stated one of this yearâs participants.
    Speakers addressed such topics as "Creating Appetizing and Appealing Metabolic Meals" and "Study Design Aspects of the Outpatient Feeding Study."  Monday and Tuesday were devoted to learning labs where guest lecturers shared knowledge, and participants practiced techniques in specially designed exercises.
    One of the most popular  labs was "Solving Problems and Working Effectively,"  presented by Steven Robichaux, of the LSU College of Business Administration.  Anita Sawyer, R.D., of the Metabolic Kitchen spoke Sunday on quality assurance procedures.  The workshop closed with a demonstration by  Pennington's own culinary expert, Kelly Patrick.
    Participants earned continuing education credits from the American Dietetic Association while gaining valuable hands-on experience.
    The program was not all work, however; plenty of time was allowed for networking and leisure activities. Tuesday's after-hour activities included a trip to Nottoway Plantation for a tour and dinner.  Then on Wednesday, ten of the workshop participants took a tour of the Atchafalaya Basin swamp in Breaux Bridge where they experienced Louisiana culture, cuisine, and even got to see an alligator! Everyone agrees that this year's workshop was a fabulous success!

-Jennifer Guillory
